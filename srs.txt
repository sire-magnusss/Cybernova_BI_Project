Overview
CyberNova Analytics Ltd is a Gaborone-based AI cybersecurity start-up serving SMEs, financial institutions, and government agencies across Southern Africa through intelligent threat monitoring, predictive maintenance, and digital transformation services. Its flagship product an AI-powered Cyber Assistant is central to both its technical offering and commercial identity.
As the assigned sales analytics consultant, I am developing a Product Sales Data Analysis System to evaluate the online effectiveness of CyberNova's software solutions through IIS web server log analysis. My approach applies CRISP-DM as the primary framework, with CRISP-DM 2.0 layered on top to accommodate the iterative relationship between synthetic data generation, EDA, and analytical refinement. The system delivers role-specific business intelligence to three stakeholders Sales, Marketing, and Management through a structured, repeatable BI pipeline.

Product to be delivered to the client
A Business Intelligence Dashboard System comprising: a synthetic IIS W3C Extended Log Format dataset simulating realistic Southern African web traffic; a modular Python pipeline performing ETL, IP resolution, EDA, and behavioural segmentation; and a live-refreshing three-tab Streamlit dashboard tailored to Sales, Marketing, and Management audiences with dynamic filtering, statistical summaries, and five chart types designed for repeatable monthly execution.

Client Requirements
Functional Requirements
ID	Requirement	Category
FR1	Generate ≥1,000-row synthetic IIS W3C log dataset (CSV/Excel): timestamps, Southern African IPs, service URIs, HTTP methods, status codes, bytes, user agents. Must reflect realistic patterns peak-hour clustering, repeat visits, crawlers, 404 errors.	Data generation
FR2	Ingest and preprocess the log file: parse all fields, resolve IPs to countries, handle malformed records, load a clean DataFrame. Report records processed, cleaned, and dropped.	ETL pipeline
FR3	Perform EDA: univariate distributions, bivariate country × service analysis, hourly/daily time series decomposition, and anomaly detection. All outputs include interpretive commentary.	Exploratory analysis
FR4	Segment traffic into four behavioural tiers: high-intent (demo/event pages), product-curious (AI/prototype pages), general browsers (index/about), bots/crawlers (user-agent or pattern detection). Report volumes per country.	Segmentation
FR5	Simulate a real-time log feed via file-watch or polling mechanism that detects new entries and refreshes all dashboard metrics without manual reload.	Real-time BI
FR6	Geographic demand analysis: ranked bar chart and choropleth of total and high-intent requests by country. Identify top 5 markets; flag underperforming regions against expansion targets.	Geographic BI
FR7	Service demand ranking across all URIs. Isolate AI Cyber Assistant traffic as a standalone flagship KPI with a growth trend indicator.	Product analytics
FR8	Three-tab Streamlit dashboard — Sales: trends, warm leads, conversion, reach. Marketing: heatmap, service gaps, segmentation. Management: KPIs, AI traction, executive summary.	BI dashboard
FR9	Sidebar filtering across all tabs: date range, country, service type, HTTP status. All visuals and statistics update reactively.	UX / interactivity
FR10	Warm lead tracking: quantify /scheduledemo.php and /event.php entries separately as highest-intent funnel interactions. Report count, country split, time trend, and rate as % of total traffic.	Sales analytics
FR11	Minimum five chart types: bar, line/area, pie/donut, scatter, geographic. All charts include titles, axis labels, legends, and annotations.	Visual reporting
FR12	Descriptive statistics per country, service, and time period: mean, median, standard deviation, totals. Correlation analysis of time-of-day vs service type to identify peak engagement windows.	Statistical analysis

3.	The requirements specification is limited to two pages as stipulated in the CET333 assessment brief, constraining the depth of formal documentation at this stage..

Evaluation
The system will be evaluated across three dimensions. 
1.	First, against functional requirements (FR1–FR12): each requirement will be tested individually  verifying log format compliance, pipeline accuracy, EDA output validity, segmentation correctness, real-time feed behaviour, chart rendering, and filter reactivity. 
2.	Second, against business goals: outputs will be assessed for traceability to CyberNova's eight documented business goals, with the system considered successful when at least six goals are demonstrably addressed by the analytical outputs. 
3.	Third, against analytical success criteria: where predictive modelling is implemented (FR13), I will evaluate and compare candidate approaches specifically simple linear regression and moving average smoothing selecting the most appropriate based on forecast accuracy and interpretability given the dataset characteristics. 
Segmentation quality will be assessed by verifying that the four behavioural tiers produce meaningfully distinct traffic profiles. Client evaluation occurs at Meeting 3, where I will demonstrate the working prototype against all agreed requirements and obtain formal sign-off confirming the system meets CyberNova's stated needs.
